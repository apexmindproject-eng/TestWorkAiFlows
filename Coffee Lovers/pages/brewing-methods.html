Brewing Methods

Explore different ways to brew your perfect cup of coffee. Each method brings out unique flavors and characteristics from the coffee beans.

Espresso

Espresso is a concentrated coffee brewed by forcing hot water through finely-ground coffee under high pressure. It forms the base for many coffee drinks such as lattes and cappuccinos.

Pour Over

The pour-over method involves manually pouring hot water over coffee grounds in a filter, allowing the coffee to slowly drip into a carafe or cup. This method highlights the coffee's nuanced flavors.

French Press

A French press uses a plunger and mesh filter to steep coffee grounds in hot water before pressing them down, resulting in a rich and full-bodied coffee.

Cold Brew

Cold brew coffee is made by steeping coarsely ground coffee in cold water for a long period, usually 12-24 hours, yielding a smooth, less acidic beverage served chilled.
