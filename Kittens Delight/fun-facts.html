Fun Facts About Kittens

  • Kittens are born blind

    Kittens open their eyes about 7–10 days after birth and rely heavily on their sense of smell and touch during their early days.

  • Purring is a way to communicate

    Kittens purr when they’re content, but purring can also be a sign of pain or stress, serving as a communication tool with their mother or humans.

  • Whiskers help kittens navigate

    A kitten’s whiskers are highly sensitive and help them measure spaces and detect nearby objects, aiding in navigation especially in low light.

  • Kittens have retractable claws

    Just like adult cats, kittens can retract their claws to keep them sharp and protect their paws while walking.

  • Kittens sleep a lot

    Kittens can sleep 16-20 hours per day to support their rapid growth and development during the first few weeks and months.
